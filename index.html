The History of Women in Tech

builtByWomen_ was designed to celebrate women programmers, developers, and builders in tech - like Ada Lovelace - with the hope to encourage future generations of women to feel more at home in the industry.

Ada Lovelace

Ada Lovelace was an English 19th century mathemetician who is famous for being the first computer programmer. That's pretty impressive, seeing as computers as we know them weren't invented for another 100 years. You can read more Lovelace's work and her story below!

Katherine Johnson

Katherine Johnson was an American mathemetician who was part of the U.S. space program for more than 30 years. Her calculations were critical to the success of the first and subsequent crewed spaceflights. You can read more about Johnson's work and her story below!

Find out more!

Radia Perlman

Radia Perlman is a pioneering American computer programmer, known as the "Mother of the Internet". Her work made it possible for networks to grow efficiently, playing a critical role in the expansion and reliability of modern internet systems.
