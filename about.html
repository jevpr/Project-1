About

The idea for builtByWomen_ came not long after the annual Ada Lovelace Day on the 8th October 2024. As the first computer programmer, Ada is an inspirational figure for women across the tech industry.

Lovelace was born in the mid-1800s and contributed inportant insights to the world of computing. Her annotations on the paper Elements of Charles Babbage's Analytical Machine were forerunners to the transition from calculation to computation. For that reason, she has been called "a prophet of the computer age".

Find out more about Ada Lovelace here.

Almost 200 years later, we still use some of the same logic that Ada implemented when creating the first ever programme designed for computers. There's even a programming language named after her.

builtByWomen_ was designed to celebrate women programmers, developers, and builders in tech - like Ada Lovelace - with the hope to encourage future generations of women to feel more at home in the industry.
